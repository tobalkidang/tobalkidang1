Natural and Historical Sites in the Philippines

The Manila Cathedral is a great testament of the Spanish legacy and socio-cultural influence on the Philippines.

Tourism ins an important sector for the Philippine Economy. In 2019, the travel and tourism industry contributed 12.7% to the coutry's GDP. The country is known for having a wealth in biodiversity and beautiful natural spots. Its beaches, heritage towns and monuments, mountains, rainforests, islands and diving spots are among the country's most popular tourist destinations. The country's rich historical and cultural heritage, including its festivals and indigenous traditions, are also popular attractions. Popular destinations among tourists are Cebu, Palawan, Siargao, Boracay, Bohol, and many more.
As of 2015, almost five million Filipinos were employed in the tourism sector. The GDP benefited from P228 billion from foreign tourists, a quarter of which generated by Boracay. The country attracted around five million visitors in 2015. In 2017, arrivals peaked at 6.6 million visitors.
Beach tourism is currently the most significant tourist attractor in the Philippines. Philippine beaches are ranked highly in international travel rankings for their natural beauty. Substatian drawback in the Philippine tourism industry is due to undeveloped infrastructure and terrorism.

Research and Education Tourism

Due to the diverse number of flora and fauna in the country, the Philippines is a major hub for researchers from around the world. Some of the Earth's most important environmental corridors are located in the Philippines such as the Coral Triangle, the most important aquatic biodiversity hotspot in the world. Places such as the Sibuyan Island, Mt. Mantalingajan, Dinagat Islands, Central Panay Range, Verde Island Passage, Tubbataha Reef, Turtle Islands, and much more are also visited by these researchers. The country is also a hotspot for Anthropology and Archaeology due to places such as Tabon Cave, Callao Cave, Lanao Del Sur, Cebu and Ifugao.
Language schools are also visited by nationals from countries such as Taiwan, Vietnam, Thailand, and S. Korea.

Boracay Beach

The Boracay Beach is perhaps the most popular beach resort in the Philippines. Boracay is a major tourist hub in the Philipines, attracting tourists from within and outside the country. Boracay is an island, about 7 kilometers long and 1 kilometer wide, located in Western Visayas. Boracays waas awarded as the 2012 Best Island in the World by the international travel magazine Travel + Leisure. In 2014, the island was placed on top of the "Best Islands in the World" list published by the International Magazine Conde Nast Traveler/
In 2018, the government decreed a 6-month closure to rehabilitate and renovate the island's obsolete sewage system. It was reopened in October of the same year. It was closed again in response to the Covid-19 pandemic.

Rizal Park

Liwasang Rizal, also known as Luneta, is a historic urban park in Ermita, Manila. It is one of the largest urban park in Asia, covering an area of almost 60 hectares. The site on wherre the park is situated was originally known as Bagumbayan during the Spanish Colonial Period. It is adjacent to the walled city of Intramuros. This park is where Rizal was executed, trigerring the Philippine Revolution that expelled the Spanish from the country. And look at that photobomber behind the monument.

Banaue Rice Terraces

The Rice Terraces of the Philippine Cordilleras are the oldest and most extensive in the world. These terraces were carved using rudimentary tools. Many of these terraces are falling into disrepair due to lack of maintainace and growingly worse climate conditions. The rice terraces are a major tourist destination in places such as Ifugao and Mountain Province. The five clusters inscribed as part of the Rice Terraces of the Philippine Cordilleras are Batad, Hungduan, Mayoyao Central, and Nagacadan. Batad and Bangaan are under the jurisdiction of the Municipality of Banaue but are not called the Banaue Rice Terraces.

The Waters

The Philippines is encased from every coast in what is called the Coral Triange This area holds an immense importance to marine ecosystem, holding an exeedingly sizable number of marine species in just a small area. Its biological resources categorize as a global hotspot of marine biodiversity that contains more than 76% of the world's shallow reef-building coral species, 37% of the world's fishes, 50% of razor clams, six out of seven of the world's sea turtles, and the largest mangrove forest in the world. Sadly, the area is caught between human activity and is aggressively exploited. The very heart of the Triagle called the Verde Island Passage is under immense pressure due to human activity and exploitation.

Biodiversity

The wildlife of the Philippines includes a significant number of endemic plant and animal species. The country's surrounding waters contain the highest level of marine biodiversity in the world as I have mentioned above. The Philippines is one of the seven megadiverse countries as well as a global biodiversity hotspot. The Philippines has among the highest rates of new species discovery with sixteen new species of mammals discovered in the last ten years alone.

Chocolate Hills of Bohol

The Chocolate Hills is a geological formation in Bohol. There are over a thousand of these hills spread over and area of fifty square kilometers. They are covered in green grass but the chocolate interior is revealed come summer. Chocolates from these hills taste like Hershey's dark chocolate but the public is not advised to consume it due to concerns that people may eat the hills flat, and there'd be no more Chocolate hill left. In 2016, five people were arrested trying to smuggle 12 tons of chocolate out of Bohol to Cagayan De Oro city. In response to that incident, the governor decided to close the area from tourist activity for six months.

Magellan's Cross

This cross was planted by Ferdinand Magellan upon arriving in Cebu on the 17th of March 1521. This cross is housed in a chapel next to the Basilica Menor del Santo Nino on Magallanes Street in front of the city centre of Cebu City. A sign below the cross states that the original cross is encased inside the wooden cross made of tindalo wood. This is to protect the original cross from people chipping parts for souveneir in the belief that the crosspossesses magical powers like the ones Harry Potter has. Some believe that the original cross has been destroyed or that the cross is a replica planted by the Spaniards years later.
